EDM Mania Hits India

January 2, 2016January 2, 2016

Looking back in the history, India has rarely seen international live music. Due to low ticket prices and high production costs, India has never been an attractive destination for the greatest international musicians. But that seems to change in the coming times. We have been seeing some great performances from some of the greatest EDM artists in the world from Afrojack to David Guetta in the past couple of years. The popularity of Electronic Dance Music (EDM) in India is now skyrocketing. The energy, volume, lights, build-ups and drops in the EDM add up to a fantastic experience.

These days many more new artists lining up in our country for pursuing DJ as their career choice.
Most of the credit to such transition goes to the majority of population below the age of 28 years and the ever increasing urbanization. Also, a major reason for such change is the exploding nightlife. Nightclubs are now packed with teenagers letting their hair down and grooving to the beats of music.
Today, EDM is a well considered music genre in India and a DJ has become a necessity for every event that involves groups and entertainment. Just about eight years ago, in 2007, the total capacity of EDM or any dance festivals in India was about a 10,000 people. Today, the number has shot up to more than 3,25,000 and it is simply growing with every passing day.

Thanks to Sunburn which started in 2007, India today feels at home with names like Avicii, Dash Berlin, Armin Van Buuren, etc. Soon, this event will be held in Dubai and Sri Lanka too. Goa’s Sunburn festival is a mega festival that draws crowds in huge numbers. Today, Sunburn festival gives them a place to let their hair down and dance to their heart’s content.
Until the last couple of years, watching the live acts of Avicii and Hardwell were a distant dream for an EDM lover. But , now we get to experience the same performances from these artists each month. Today, we have clubs dedicated to the genre, most of them have dedicated EDM nights. The admiration, craze and ticket sales once enjoyed by the likes of Mick Jagger, Jimi Hendrix, Pink Floyd, Metallica, have in the recent past shifted towards DJs like Tiesto, David Guetta, Armin van Buuren and Fatboy Slim.

Seeing the pace of the evolution of music in India, that moment is not far when we see the Indian tours of some the great artists from the likes of Eminem to Rihanna.
No matter what the accusations of drug scenes prevailing at the EDM events are, this new cultural change in the taste of the youth is here to stay for many years to come.
